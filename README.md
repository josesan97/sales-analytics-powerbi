## Project Overview
Businesses generate thousands of sales transactions every day, but raw data alone does not provide meaningful insights. This project transforms transactional sales data into an interactive Power BI dashboard that helps decision-makers monitor performance, identify business opportunities, and support strategic decisions.
The dashboard was designed following Business Intelligence best practices, combining data modeling, DAX calculations, and interactive visualizations to create an intuitive reporting experience for executives and business users.

---
## Business Problem

Sales managers often need to answer questions such as:
- Which products generate the highest revenue?
- Which categories are the most profitable?
- Which regions underperform?
- How are sales evolving over time?
- Which customer segments contribute the most to profit?

Without a centralized dashboard, answering these questions requires manual analysis across multiple spreadsheets, making decision-making slower and less reliable.

---

## Project Objectives

The primary objective of this project was to transform raw sales data into an interactive Business Intelligence solution that supports strategic and operational decision-making.
The dashboard was designed to:
- Monitor overall sales and profitability through executive KPIs.
- Track sales performance across products, categories, and regions.
- Evaluate customer segment performance and purchasing behavior.
- Identify sales trends over time.
- Compare business performance using interactive filtering and drill-down analysis.
- Present actionable insights through a clean, user-friendly dashboard experience.

## Dataset

The dashboard was developed using the **Sample Superstore** dataset, a widely used dataset for Business Intelligence and data analytics projects.

The dataset contains transactional sales information, including:
- Order Date
- Ship Date
- Customer Segment
- Product Category
- Sub-Category
- Product Name
- Sales
- Profit
- Quantity
- Discount
- Geographic Information (Region, State and City)

This information provides a solid foundation for analyzing sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, and regional trends.

---

## Data Preparation

The dataset was prepared using Power Query before building the dashboard.

Main preparation steps included:

- Verified data types.
- Created a dedicated Calendar table.
- Established relationships between tables.
- Built a star-schema data model.
- Created calculated measures using DAX.
- Organized the model for efficient reporting.
---

## Data Model

The report follows a relational data model centered on the Orders table, supported by a Calendar table for time intelligence and additional lookup tables where applicable.

### Business Insights
The analysis uncovered several business patterns that can support strategic decision-making and future business growth.

#### 1. Technology Is the Main Revenue Driver
Technology generated the highest sales among all product categories, making it the strongest contributor to overall revenue. Continued investment in this category could help sustain long-term sales growth.

---

#### 2. Profitability Differs Significantly by Region
The West region achieved the highest profit, followed by the East region, while the South and Central regions lagged behind. These differences suggest opportunities to review regional sales strategies, pricing, and operational efficiency.

---

#### 3. Sales Show Positive Growth Over Time
Monthly sales trends indicate a general upward trajectory throughout the analyzed period. The dashboard also reports a **47.16% Year-over-Year Sales Growth**, reflecting strong business performance compared to the previous year.

---

#### 4. Fourth Quarter Represents the Strongest Sales Period
Quarterly analysis shows that Q4 consistently generated the highest sales volume, suggesting a seasonal increase in customer demand. Planning inventory and marketing campaigns around this period could maximize business performance.

---

#### 5. Product Performance Is Highly Concentrated
Product analysis revealed that a relatively small number of products account for a significant share of total sales, while several products consistently underperform. Regularly reviewing low-performing products can help optimize the product portfolio and improve profitability.

---

### Business Recommendations
Based on the dashboard findings, potential business actions include:
- Increase focus on high-performing products.
- Review products with low profitability.
- Optimize discount strategies.
- Strengthen performance in underperforming regions.
- Monitor KPIs regularly through interactive reporting.
